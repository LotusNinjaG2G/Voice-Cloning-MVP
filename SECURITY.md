# Security Policy & Safeguards Disclosure (SecureVoice PoC)

This document details the security posture, risk mitigation controls, architectural choices, and social-engineering protections designed into the SecureVoice Proof-of-Concept.

---

## 1. Safety & Security Safeguards

### Consent Gating by Design
- Voice generation is strictly gated behind **explicit participant consent**.
- Consent status is verified on every generation request.
- Gating checks check for:
  1. Signed consent flag.
  2. Complete recorded consent audio file matching the active capture session.
  3. Active, verified live-recording voice sample.
- If consent is revoked or has expired, all generation attempts are blocked instantly by the FastAPI backend before contacting any speech synthesizer.

### Live-Capture Only (Upload Restrictions)
- To prevent actors from abusing this app using scraped or stolen pre-recorded audio, **generic audio uploads are blocked**.
- Drag-and-drop, file upload boxes, and link-based audio imports are intentionally omitted from the frontend.
- The backend matches the unique `capture_session_id` generated by the browser's `MediaRecorder` during the continuous recording. If a submitted audio sample contains a session ID that does not map back to a verified, completed consent statement recording, it is rejected with HTTP 400.

### Content Filtering Scope & Limitations
- Prior to calling the voice provider, the input text script is analyzed against safety policy rules to block potential fraud templates.
- **Blocked Terms / Patterns**:
  - Financial authorization terms (e.g. "authorize payment", "bank transfer").
  - Urgent prompts ("send money immediately", "transfer cash now").
  - Credentials and verification factors (OTPs, passwords, 2FA codes, PINs).
  - Public figure impersonations (e.g. "President Biden", "Elon Musk").
  - Inter-session impersonation: Preventing any selected participant from introducing themselves as a name not on the session's active participant roster, or introducing themselves as another participant in the same session.
- *Limitation Note*: Content filtering is regex/keyword-based in this PoC and should be supplemented in production with robust LLM-based semantic safety moderation and active classifiers.

### Embedded Audio Disclosure / Watermarking
- Every generated WAV audio clip has a distinct double-beep tone sequence prepended.
- The mock voice provider includes a spoken audio disclaimer stating: *"Synthetic voice demo. Fallback demo audio. This does not clone the voice."*
- These markers ensure the audio is immediately recognizable as synthetic, mitigating deceptive social engineering.

## 2. Data Deletion & Retention Policy

### SQLite Auto-Expiry (TTL)
- Sessions are initialized with a Time-to-Live (TTL) configured by the host (default: 24 hours).
- A periodic cleanup task running in the backend automatically queries, deletes, and purges database records and physical audio files (consent recordings, voice samples, and generated WAV files) from disk once the TTL expires.

### Host-Initiated Immediate Purge
- The host has a prominent **End & Purge Session** control on the dashboard.
- Clicking this instantly terminates the session, drops all tables/relationships from SQLite, and physically deletes the audio folders from disk using synchronous server filesystem commands.

## 3. Social-Engineering Threat Model

AI voice cloning introduces unprecedented capability for social-engineering attacks:
1. **CEO/Executive Impersonation**: Attacking accounts payable staff by mimicking executive voices to order wire transfers.
2. **Family Emergency Fraud (Vishing)**: Target victims by using cloned voices of their children or grandchildren stating they are in jail or hospitalized and need immediate funds.
3. **Voice Authentication Bypass**: Attacking IVR call centers that use voice recognition for identity verification.

---

## 4. Recommended Production Safeguards

To deploy a voice-cloning utility in a production environment, the following enterprise-grade safeguards are required:
- **Liveness Detection & Anti-Replay**: Implement deep acoustic signal processing to verify the speaker is a live human rather than a recording played back into the microphone.
- **Cryptographic Consent Signing**: Require the participant to cryptographically sign the consent transaction (e.g. using a hardware enclave or WebAuthn private keys) to create a legally binding, audit-ready record.
- **Spectral Audio Watermarking**: Embed inaudible, robust watermarks throughout the generated audio frequency spectrum that survive transcoding, cropping, and conversion.
- **Identity Verification (e.g. KYC)**: Cross-reference participant names with government IDs and live camera matching before registering voice models.
- **Jurisdictional Legal Review**: Ensure the consent collection mechanisms meet local legal frameworks (such as GDPR, CCPA, and regional biometric privacy acts).
